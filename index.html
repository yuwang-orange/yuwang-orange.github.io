About Me

I am an Applied ML Scientist with a Ph.D. in Economics from Cornell University, specializing in causal inference, machine learning, and LLM evaluation. My research bridges econometric methodology and modern ML systems, with applications in marketplace optimization, loss forecasting, and causal diagnostics.

Currently, I work as a Risk Quantitative Analyst at Capital One, where I build end-to-end ML pipelines for credit loss prediction and develop causal frameworks for model evaluation under distributional shift.

I am seeking Applied Scientist and Research Scientist roles in marketplace optimization, experimentation, and causal ML.

Research Interests

Causal Inference Machine Learning LLM Evaluation Marketplace Optimization Heterogeneous Treatment Effects Time Series Forecasting

My work centers on two themes: (1) applying causal inference and partial identification methods to ML systems — including stress testing, sensitivity analysis, and uncertainty decomposition; and (2) bridging econometric frameworks with production ML pipelines for forecasting, experimentation, and decision-making at scale.
